Exercise 1 – RSA cryptosystem:<\/b><\/h1>\n\n\n\n

The role of 2 people tries to encrypt and decrypt the message the message from public and private key.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    \n

  • Person 1 generates the public key and private key using different prime numbers<\/a><\/h3>\n
      \n
    • Generating public and private keys<\/li>\n\n\n\n
    • Pick two prime numbers, like p<\/strong><\/em> = 3 and q<\/strong><\/em> = 13<\/li>\n\n\n\n
    • Calculate n<\/strong><\/em> = p<\/strong><\/em> * q<\/strong><\/em> = 3 * 13 = 39<\/li>\n\n\n\n
    • Calculate z<\/strong><\/em> = (p<\/strong><\/em> – 1) * (q<\/strong><\/em> – 1) = (3-1) * (13 -1) = 2 * 12 = 24<\/li>\n\n\n\n
    • Choose a prime number k<\/strong><\/em>, such that k<\/strong><\/em> is co-prime to z<\/em><\/strong>, i.e…, z<\/em><\/strong> is not divisible by k<\/em><\/strong>\n
        \n
      • We have several choices for k<\/strong><\/em>: 5, 7, 11, 13, 17, 19, 23 (we cannot use 3, because 24 is divisible by that numbers). => Let’s pick k<\/em><\/strong> = 5<\/li>\n\n\n\n
      • That means the number n<\/strong><\/em> = 39 and k<\/strong><\/em> = 5 become the public key<\/strong><\/li>\n<\/ul>\n<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n<\/li>\n\n\n\n

      • Person 1 passes the public key to Person 2<\/h3>\n
          \n
        • give Person 2: n<\/strong><\/em> = 39 and k<\/strong><\/em> = 5 <\/li>\n<\/ul>\n<\/li>\n\n\n\n

        • Person 2 chooses a number between 1 and 50 as the message: Ex. Person 2 chooses 20 as the message <\/h3>\n
            \n
          • ->Plain message: P = 20<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n<\/li>\n\n\n\n

          • Person 2 encrypts the message and passes the encrypted message to Person 1<\/h3>\n
              \n
            • Calculate the private key. Here is how:\n
                \n
              • k<\/strong><\/em> * j<\/strong><\/em> = 1 (mod z<\/strong><\/em>) –> 5 * j<\/strong><\/em> = 1 (mod 24) –> (5 * j<\/strong>) \/ 24 =? (with the remainder of 1 – are only interested in the remainder)<\/li>\n\n\n\n
              • We can easily conclude that 25\/24 = 1 (with the remainder of 1), so that means j = 5, which is our secret key. We MUST NOT<\/strong> give this key away!<\/li>\n\n\n\n
              • Now, we can begin our message transmission from our browser to the server. First, the browser requests the public key from the server, which was the server sends. It sends n = 39 and k = 5 back to the browser.<\/li>\n\n\n\n
              • Now, we assume that the browser has a plain message P = 20, and it wants to encrypt it before sending it to the server.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n<\/li>\n\n\n\n
              • Let’s Encrypting the message\n
                  \n
                • Here is the formula encryption math that browser executes: P<\/strong> ^ k<\/strong> = E<\/strong> (mod n<\/strong>)<\/li>\n\n\n\n
                • 20 ^ 5 = E<\/strong> (mod 39) –> E<\/strong> = (20 ^ 5) \/ 39 = 3200000\/39 = 11<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n<\/li>\n\n\n\n

                • Person 1 decrypts the message<\/h3>\n
                    \n
                  • here is the decryption math the server executes to recover the original plain text message which the browser started with: E ^ j = P (mod n)<\/li>\n\n\n\n
                  • Plugging in the values\n
                      \n
                    • 11 ^ 5 = P (mod 39)<\/li>\n\n\n\n
                    • (11 ^ 5) \/ 39 = 161051 \/ 39 = 4129.51282051…<\/li>\n\n\n\n
                    • 4129 * 39 = 161031 <\/li>\n\n\n\n
                    • P = 161051 – 161031 = 20 <\/li>\n\n\n\n
                    • That means P<\/strong> = 20 is the exactly plain text message that the browser started with!<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n\n\n\n

                      Notes:<\/h2>\n\n\n\n

                      ‘^’ means “to the power of)<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                      P is the plain message we want to encrypt<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                      n and k are server’s public key<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                      E is our encrypted message we want to generate<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                      P: plain message<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                      j: the server’s secret key<\/p>\n\n\n\n
